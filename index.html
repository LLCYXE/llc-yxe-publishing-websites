##Getting Started with FTP ###Glossary * **FTP** – This stands for **File Transfer Protocol**. That's a way of describing files are moved from one place to another. It's how you move website files (HTML pages, images and PDFs) from your computer to your website. * **Host and Server** – For us these terms are basically interchangeable and refer to the place where your website resides. You move your files from your computer to a **server**, and the server will display them on the Internet.
##Getting Started with FTP ###Tools * **[Filezilla](http://filezilla-project.org/download.php?type=client)** - The FTP client we will be using is Filezilla. Another good alternative is [Cyberduck](https://cyberduck.io/?l=en). [![Filezilla](framework/img/workshop/filezilla-logo.png)](http://filezilla-project.org/download.php?type=client)
##Using Filezilla Your hosting service provider should provide you with these pieces of information: * FTP Address * Username * Password Enter that information here and click **Quickconnect**: ![Filezilla](framework/img/workshop/filezilla-connect.png)
##Using Filezilla The left panel shows your computer's files, the right panel shows the server's files. In the left panel, locate your workshop files (mine are on the desktop). In the right panel, locate the directory that the server displays on the domain. (If you are using Varial this is **public_html**) ![](framework/img/workshop/ftp1.png)
##Using Filezilla Select the workshop files, right click and choose **Upload** ![](framework/img/workshop/ftp2.png)
##Using Filezilla After uploading, you will see your files in the **public_html** folder on the server. ![](framework/img/workshop/ftp3.png)
##Check it out! Your site is now uploaded! Check out your domain to see the changes! If you just got your domain name, it may not have enough time to resolved to the domain name servers.
